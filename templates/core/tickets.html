DjangoCon is a Not-for-profit conference

The conference is staffed entirely by unpaid volunteers; all proceeds from ticket sales go towards the expenses of putting on the conference.

Buying your tickets early helps twice: the conference organizers get a better picture of the finances and you enjoy a lower rate. Attendance anxiety is a leading cause of emotional meltdown among conference organizers; please help fight this awful disease by booking your tickets as early as possible.

Which rate should I pay?

The short answer

If you are paying your own way to the conference, select Individual. If your employer is paying for you to attend DjangoCon, select Corporate. This is a community-supported conference; please be honest when choosing a ticket type.

Corporate rate

If you have your own company or your employer is paying for your trip (which they should): please opt for the corporate rate. Included in your ticket purchase is the warm fuzzy feeling of knowing you enabled more students to afford their attendance fee and food for the month.

Individual rate

If you use Django for personal projects and plan to attend the conference at your own expense, choose the individual rate.

Student rate

The student rate is for full-time students only and is limited in number. Students who have bought a ticket must email us a scan of a valid student ID as proof to students@djangocon.eu within 48 hours or risk their ticket being cancelled.

Academics and employees of non-profits are not considered students, and should register at one of the normal depending on whether you are paying your own way to the conference (see above).

What you're getting

Registration for the conference includes:

  • Access to all talks and sessions
  • Free lunch
  • Coffee and snacks during breaks
  • Free wifi at the conference
  • And… a beautiful t-shirt, of course

Buy tickets early

Did we mention that buying your ticket early helps the environment, cures cancer, and promotes world peace?

OK, it doesn’t do any of those things. But it does give us a clearer picture of attendance (and our finances). This helps. A lot.

Furthermore, buying your ticket before April 1st gets you an early bird discount.

Hotel and lodging

We strongly encourage you to book a room early as well. We've partnered with several hotels to provide a discounted conference rate for attendees. See our hotels page for further details.
